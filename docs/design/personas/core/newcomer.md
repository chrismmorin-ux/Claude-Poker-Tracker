# Persona — The Newcomer

**Type:** Core (end-user archetype)
**Evidence status:** PROTO — unverified
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-21

---

## Snapshot

Learned poker on a phone app or in home games. Curious about real games but scared of the vocabulary, the pace, and looking dumb. Would benefit enormously from a play-aid but is also the persona most likely to be alienated by the tool if it talks like a solver.

## Context

- **Venue:** Any — low-stakes first real games.
- **Format:** Any — probably cash at the lowest stake.
- **Skill:** Beginner.
- **Volume:** 5–15 hrs/month.
- **Device:** Phone.
- **Intent:** Learning in context.
- **Role:** Player-student.

## Goals

- Learn the vocabulary in context (what's a 3-bet? what's SPR?).
- Get "don't do this" warnings before catastrophic mistakes.
- See simple progress markers.
- Not feel dumb.

## Frustrations

- Apps assume knowledge.
- Terminology is gatekept.
- Afraid of looking like a fish using a tool at the table.

## Non-goals

- Pro-level analysis.
- High volume.

---

## Constraints

- **Time pressure:** Full at the table; low off-table.
- **Error tolerance:** High (they're learning).
- **Visibility tolerance:** Low — clutter alienates.
- **Complexity tolerance:** Very low initially; growing.
