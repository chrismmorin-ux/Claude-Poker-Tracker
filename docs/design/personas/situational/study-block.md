# Situational Sub-Persona — Study Block

**Type:** Situational (cross-persona)
**Applies to:** Scholar, Apprentice, Rounder (occasional), Hybrid (occasional), Circuit Grinder (off-day)
**Evidence status:** PROTO
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-21

---

## Snapshot

The user has a defined block of study time — 10 minutes on the subway, 30 minutes with morning coffee, an hour before a session. They want structure: a drill, a concept, a review window. Unlike live play, there's no cognitive load from the game itself; all brain cycles are available for learning.

---

## Situation trigger

- User deliberately opens the app for study (not for play-aid).
- Or: scheduled study reminder fires.
- Exits when: time is up, session starts, or user navigates away.

## Context

- **Time pressure:** None (except user's personal schedule).
- **Attention:** Focused.
- **Cognitive load:** Light to moderate — loaded with concept, not with simultaneous decisions.
- **Device:** Phone for short blocks, desktop for longer.

## Primary need

- Quick entry into a drill or review targeted at weakness or today's concept.
- Progress visible — streak, concept coverage, mastery advancement.
- Explanations for why the correct answer is correct.

## Secondary needs

- Ability to save an interesting drill for later review.
- Ability to switch drill domain (preflop ↔ postflop) without full navigation reset.

## What a surface must offer

1. **Concept-of-the-day** or targeted-weakness drill as the default path — no friction to start.
2. **Explanation after every drill**, not just a score.
3. **Streak + progress visibility** without overdoing gamification.
4. **Spaced repetition** for high-value concepts (preflop charts especially).

## What a surface must NOT do

- Force full-flow navigation before the first drill.
- Score without explanation.
- Feel like work rather than study.
