## Feature summary (as proposed)

Current card-entry flow (board: flop / turn / river; villain showdown: 2 cards × N villains) requires owner to stare at the phone in landscape for ~10–15 seconds per board to tap rank + suit chips. The cost is threefold:

1. Breaks table presence — eyes off the table while opponents may be acting or revealing tells.
2. Telegraphs that the owner is recording — extended phone-staring while public cards are revealed is conspicuous (tension with [H-PLT04 socially discreet](../heuristics/poker-live-table.md#h-plt04--socially-discreet)).
3. Slow enough that hands sometimes go unrecorded when action moves fast — owner has self-reported losing hands to entry speed.

**Proposed mechanic** (founder-ratified 2026-05-11):

- Push-and-hold a button (one of: in-picker, floating mic, or both — Gate 4 decision). While held, the device listens via Web Speech API.
- Owner speaks the cards: "ace of hearts, jack of spades, ten of clubs." Phonetic variants ("ace o hearts" / "ace hearts" / "ace of harts") snap to canonical cards.
- Release → constrained parser maps transcript to a set of cards → confirmation chips render → single tap commits.
- Three founder ratifications binding:
  - **R1 — Web Speech only.** No Whisper, no cloud transcription, no escalation path. Free, on-device, zero infra. If Web Speech is too noisy for live use, the feature is **dropped**, not upgraded.
  - **R2 — Scope locked.** Board cards (3 + 1 + 1) and villain showdown cards (2 each) only. Hole cards (owner is already looking at phone) and actions / sizes (separate, much larger problem) are OUT.
  - **R3 — Ship-or-drop spike.** Live-table validation passes the kill criteria → ship behind a flag. Fails → rip out. No incremental "make it 5% better" follow-ups.

**Tension with DISC-03 (2026-04-22 REJECTED):** Voice input for live entry was rejected on 2026-04-22 in the initial gap-list triage. Rationale at the time: "PWA voice-capture APIs unreliable across Chrome / Safari / Android variants; low-confidence benefit at M-effort. **Re-open if platform capabilities mature + user demand surfaces.**" This audit is the owner-driven re-open: (a) owner has confirmed demand by name (2026-05-11), (b) scope has been radically narrowed from "live entry" (all actions + all cards) to "cards only, board + villain showdown only," (c) the ship-or-drop framing accepts that browser variance may kill the feature outright. The re-open is therefore *consistent* with the rejection conditions, not in violation of them.

## Output 1 — Scope classification

**Primary classification:** **Surface addition** (per LIFECYCLE.md). VCE introduces a new affordance (push-to-talk button) on two existing surfaces (TableView board entry, ShowdownView villain card entry) plus new shared components (parser, confirmation chips, hook). It is *not* a system-coherence audit (no cross-zone design language gap), *not* a cross-surface journey change (no flow alteration — the entry endpoint is unchanged), and *not* a product-line expansion (sidebar is not in scope).

**Secondary classifications:**

- **New interaction modality.** Voice is the first non-tap, non-keyboard input modality in the codebase. LIFECYCLE.md applying-gates table line `Add new menu action → 1, 4, 5 (Gate 2 if adds new interaction pattern)` — Gate 2 is therefore triggered by modality alone, independent of the YELLOW verdict.
- **Feature-flagged spike.** Lives behind a Settings toggle, OFF by default. Kill criteria explicit. Code may be ripped out (R3) — implies the surface artifact must include a "drop path" section in addition to the standard sections.
- **iOS-Safari fallback required.** Web Speech API support in iOS Safari is partial — `webkitSpeechRecognition` exists but background-tab + permission UX differs from Chrome. Fallback policy (silent-disable vs grayed-button-with-explanation) is a Gate 4 decision flag.
- **Re-opening of REJECTED discovery DISC-03.** The audit documents the re-open and updates DISC-03's status (REJECTED → RE-OPENED-AS-WS-181). This is governance hygiene — REJECTED discoveries should not be silently revived.

**NOT a hole-card entry change.** Founder ratification R2 binding. Hole cards stay on the existing tap picker.
**NOT an action-entry change.** Actions, sizes, raises, folds are explicitly out. HE-16 (voice input for actions) remains Proposed and is *not* served by this workstream. WS-181 does not block HE-16; the two could converge later if VCE ships and the grammar generalizes — but R3 (ship-or-drop, no incremental follow-ups) makes that explicitly not a path.
**NOT a sidebar / online-capture change.** Ignition online-capture already auto-captures cards via WebSocket. VCE serves the *live, manual-entry* surfaces only.
**NOT engine code.** Zero impact on `src/utils/exploitEngine/`, `src/utils/rangeEngine/`, `src/utils/pokerCore/`. VCE is input-layer only — it produces the same card objects the existing tap picker produces, fed through the same downstream pipeline.

### Persona sufficiency check (Stage A pre-roundtable)

**Existing personas cover the operators.** No new core or situational persona is required for VCE. The cast is sufficient.

**One sub-situation worth flagging for Stage A:** the *board-reveal moment* — between hero's preflop bet and the dealer fanning the flop — is structurally hybrid. It's "mid-hand" by Chris's persona definitions (hand is live, hero is in the hand), but it's *not* "mid-hand decision pressure" (action isn't on hero, hero has 10–30s of operator headroom). The existing personas treat board-reveal as a moment *inside* mid-hand. VCE's operating model is closer to "the breath between actions" — closer to a 5–15s focused-input window than the 1.5s glance budget of mid-hand-chris. Gate 2 Stage A should verify whether a "board-reveal Chris" sub-persona earns its keep or whether between-hands-chris adequately covers the moment. **Initial reading: between-hands-chris covers it with a clarifying note; no new persona warranted.** Flagged as a Stage A check.

**Identification: this is a YELLOW signal on Stage A** — not a structural gap, but a sub-persona ambiguity worth resolving before Gate 4 surface authoring.

### Proposed new JTBD (candidates for Gate 2 ratification)

The existing atlas has no JTBD for **card entry by voice** as a discrete outcome. HE-16 talks about voice for actions; the implicit JTBD for card entry today is "tap rank + suit chips in CardPicker" — never named because it's the only way it's done. VCE creates a new outcome the atlas should name.

| Candidate | Statement |
|---|---|
| **`HE-NEW-VCE-01`** Enter newly revealed cards hands-free | "When public cards are revealed (flop dealt, turn dealt, river dealt, or villain rolls hole cards at showdown), I want to record them into the app without looking down at the phone for 10+ seconds — so I stay table-present and don't draw attention to the act of recording." |
| **`HE-NEW-VCE-02`** Recover from a misheard card in one tap | "When the voice parser hears the wrong card (homophone, ambient noise, slurred phrase), I want to correct that single card without re-entering the whole board — so a partial mishearing doesn't escalate to a full re-entry." |

Gate 2 Stage B explicitly tests whether HE-NEW-VCE-01 is genuinely a distinct outcome from HE-14 (discreet entry) and HE-16 (voice for actions), or whether it's better captured as a sub-outcome of HE-14 with VCE as the implementation. **Initial reading: HE-NEW-VCE-01 is distinct enough to warrant its own JTBD entry** — HE-14 frames the discretion goal; HE-NEW-VCE-01 frames the modality-specific outcome (eyes-off-phone, hands-free during reveal). HE-NEW-VCE-02 is more clearly a sub-JTBD of HE-12 (undo / repair a miskeyed action) and may not need a new entry — Gate 4 can fold it into HE-12 with a "voice-correction sub-flow" note.

## Observations without fixes (carried forward)

1. **Single-user product reality.** Chris is the sole verified user. The 13×4 = 52 phonetic-variant grammar can be tuned to Chris's specific pronunciation patterns. This is a forward-looking observation, not a Gate 1 finding — Gate 4 may discuss per-user grammar tuning vs static grammar.
2. **R3 implies surface-artifact lifecycle has a "drop path."** Unlike most surfaces that ship and stay shipped, `voice-card-entry.md` must include a post-validation section documenting either ship-result or drop-rationale. Format: section pre-authored at Gate 4 with `Status: spike — pending validation`, updated at Gate 5 close to one of `Status: shipped — flag ON` / `Status: shipped — flag OFF (owner opt-in)` / `Status: archived — dropped, see [/decide ADR]`.
3. **Phonetic grammar is the load-bearing primitive.** The user-perceived quality of VCE is approximately `f(parser accuracy under typical SPL)` — not Web Speech raw accuracy, which is irrelevant if the parser snaps common mishearings to the right card. Gate 4 should specify the grammar table in full (13 ranks × 4 suits + "of" + tolerated alternates) and require it be code-reviewable, not buried in regex.
4. **iOS-Safari is the only known infeasibility risk.** Chrome / Edge / Android Chrome path is well-understood. iOS Safari historically has aggressive timeouts on continuous recognition + permission UX that interrupts hold-to-talk. Spike may discover iOS is unusable — that's a Gate 5 finding, not a Gate 1 blocker.
5. **No baseline measurement exists today.** WS-181 kill criteria reference "faster than current tap baseline on ≥80% of trials." There is no instrumented timing of the existing CardPicker entry flow. Gate 4 should specify how the baseline is measured (manual stopwatch over N sessions? Playwright timing harness?) before kill criteria can be evaluated.
